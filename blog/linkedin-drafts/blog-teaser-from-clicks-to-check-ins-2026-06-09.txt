Your website had thousands of visitors last month. Only 7% completed a booking.

Most hotel operators would respond by buying more traffic. The data says that is the wrong move.

We worked with a hotel marketing manager who did exactly that. Strong traffic, consistent 7% booking rate, growing ad budget. Bookings barely moved.

A data analyst went through 100,000 searches and found the answer sitting in two numbers. Desktop users were converting at 8.3%. Mobile users at 6.0%. Standalone room bookings closed at 9.3%. Bundled packages — the ones with breakfast, spa access, and airport transfers — converted at just 4.1%.

The problem was not traffic volume. It was which visitors they were targeting, and what the booking experience looked like when those visitors arrived.

Once the team stopped mixing packages with standalone rooms, rebuilt the mobile checkout, and focused campaigns on the segments that actually converted, the booking rate crossed 10%. Cost per booking dropped 28%. No extra ad spend needed.

If your clicks are strong but your conversions are not, the answer is probably not more traffic.
