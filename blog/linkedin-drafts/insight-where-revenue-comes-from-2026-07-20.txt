Your discount campaign did not make customers spend more. They just came once.

Ahmad ran a household and lifestyle store in Klang Valley. Over 12 months, he ran different campaigns — some with deep discounts, some with broader product selections, some timed around seasonal peaks. He wanted to know what actually worked.

When we analysed the data, one finding stood out clearly: average spending per visit stayed between RM88 and RM96 across every campaign. Customers who came in through his deepest discount promotions spent exactly the same per visit as customers who paid full price.

Discounts did not move basket size at all.

What changed between campaigns was loyalty. Customers attracted by heavy discounts rarely returned. Customers who came through campaigns with a wider product variety became repeat buyers.

Ahmad's best-performing campaign was not his biggest discount offer. It was his widest product selection, with a modest promotion attached.

Check your own past campaigns: do the customers your promotions bring in ever come back? If most of them don't, you are spending money to buy one-time transactions — not to build a customer base.

That is an expensive habit to run every month.
