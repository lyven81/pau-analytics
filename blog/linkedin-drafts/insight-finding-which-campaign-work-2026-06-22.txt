Email is probably your most-used marketing channel.

It might also be your worst performer.

A marketing manager analysed 50 past campaigns across Email, Social Media, Display Ads, and Paid Search. He had been running Email the most — 14 campaigns — because it felt safe and inexpensive.

The data showed it was the weakest channel in every single run.

Display Ads and Paid Search delivered 25% higher customer response rates. Consistently. Social Media sat in the middle. Email — despite getting the most budget and attention — came last every time.

The most uncomfortable finding: he had been measuring cost-per-send, not cost-per-result. Email looked cheap. But it was actually the most expensive channel per conversion.

Two other things the data revealed:

November campaigns produced nearly double the response of September campaigns. Timing matters more than most business owners think.

High Value and New Customer segments outperformed Deal Seekers by a wide margin. Chasing inactive customers cost him conversions from people who were actually ready to buy.

If your marketing budget is going mostly to Email, check your results by channel before the next campaign goes out.

The answer might change where you spend next quarter.
