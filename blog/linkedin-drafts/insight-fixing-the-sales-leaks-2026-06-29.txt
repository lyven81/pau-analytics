Your mobile customers convert better than your desktop ones.

That surprises most online retailers. The assumption is that mobile is for browsing. Desktop is where people actually buy. But an analysis of 500,000 shopping sessions from a Malaysian online fashion retailer told a different story.

Mobile shoppers were more likely to add items to cart — and more likely to complete checkout — than desktop users. The retailer had been investing in her desktop experience, assuming that was where serious buyers spent their time. She was focused on the wrong channel.

The more striking finding was this: email subscribers converted far better than social media visitors, organic search traffic, or anyone who typed the URL directly. Her Instagram followers browsed. Her email list bought.

She was spending time and budget growing her social presence, hoping those followers would eventually convert. Meanwhile, the one channel actually delivering buyers was getting the least attention.

Two quick things to check in your own business: which traffic source is actually converting to sales (not clicks, not sessions — actual sales), and whether your mobile checkout experience is as smooth as your desktop one.

If you have not looked at either in the past six months, the answer is probably not what you think.
