Your Christmas campaign is probably running three weeks too long.

Most businesses spread their holiday budget across eight weeks — mid-November to early January. The logic sounds reasonable. The holiday season is eight weeks, so you market for eight weeks.

We looked at eight years of holiday engagement data to find out when audiences actually pay attention to Christmas content.

The real window is three weeks.

Weeks 50, 51, and 52 — the final three weeks of December — that is where engagement peaks, every single year, without exception. From January through October: almost nothing. Mid-November: a slow rise. Then the last three weeks explode.

One retail mall marketing manager used to spread his RM300,000 Christmas campaign budget evenly across eight weeks. After seeing the data, he shifted 60% of his budget into those final three weeks. Mid-November became a light warm-up at 20% of budget. Early January became zero spend.

His total budget did not change. It just stopped going to weeks when nobody was listening.

If your business runs seasonal marketing — Christmas, Hari Raya, Chinese New Year, school holidays — your peak window is probably shorter and sharper than your campaign calendar assumes.
