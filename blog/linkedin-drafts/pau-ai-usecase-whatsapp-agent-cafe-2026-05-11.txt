Category: whatsapp-agent / café
Published: 11 May 2026

---

Amir runs a café in Cheras with one kitchen and eight tables.

By 9am on a weekday, his WhatsApp already has 14 messages. Six are asking about the lunch menu. Three want to know if he delivers. Two are regulars who want to reserve a table. One is a complaint from yesterday he has not seen yet. Two are asking what time he closes.

He is in the kitchen. He cannot reply.

By noon, three of those customers have found somewhere else to eat. The complaint has been sitting unread for 19 hours.

Now the WhatsApp agent handles all of this before Amir touches his phone.

Here is what it does on his behalf:

- Sends the set lunch menu with pricing the moment a customer asks — RM12 nasi campur, RM8 economy rice, RM5 for drinks
- Confirms table reservations for up to 8 tables and sends a reminder one hour before the booking
- Tells delivery customers which platforms he is on and links to his Grab Food page
- Flags the complaint to Amir as a priority message so it does not get buried

Amir still cooks. He still greets his regulars. But 90% of the WhatsApp traffic is handled before it reaches him.

For a one-person operation running six days a week, that is several hours of daily back-and-forth resolved automatically.
