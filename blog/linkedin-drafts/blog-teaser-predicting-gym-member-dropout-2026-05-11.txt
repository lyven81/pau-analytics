Most gym owners know when a member has quit.

Knowing who is about to quit — that is a different skill entirely.

We analysed 200 gym members across visit frequency, workout duration, calories burned, and age. One signal predicted dropout with a correlation of -0.85. That is almost as strong as a signal gets in a small business dataset.

It was not age. It was not how hard someone trained. It was simply how often they showed up.

Here is what surprised Marcus, the gym owner in this case study: age was almost completely useless as a predictor. He had been assuming younger members were more committed and older ones more likely to drift away. The data showed training habits were 30 times more predictive than age.

The most useful finding was a specific group: members who came in 8 to 12 times per month. For them, losing just three visits in a week was an early warning sign — not a confirmed departure, but a moment to act. Members in this window, if contacted early, came back. Left alone, they were gone within a month.

If you run a gym, yoga studio, swim school, or any membership business, your data is probably already showing you who is at risk. The question is whether you are reading it.
