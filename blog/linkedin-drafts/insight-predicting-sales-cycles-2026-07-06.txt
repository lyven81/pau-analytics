Your slow weeks are not random. They are on a schedule.

Most e-commerce owners plan their stock and promotions based on memory — last year's peak, last month's dip. They know the pattern exists. They just cannot pin it down.

We analysed 34,500 orders from a Malaysian e-commerce seller over two years. The model explained 78% of weekly revenue variation — and the driver was not marketing spend, product range, or trend. It was season.

Peak Season weeks add an average of RM8,430 in revenue compared to a moderate week. Slow Season weeks cut it by RM7,914. That is a RM16,344 swing between a peak and a slow week — and it repeats on the same schedule, every cycle.

The seller was stocking up at the wrong time and running promotions when sales were already strong. Flipping both decisions — stock before peaks, promote during slow weeks — changed the outcome without spending more.

Check your own sales data for the last 12 months. Mark the weeks your revenue dropped below average. Then mark the ones that climbed. If you see a pattern, you probably have a predictable cycle — and you can plan for it.

Check your own numbers. The pattern is probably already there.
