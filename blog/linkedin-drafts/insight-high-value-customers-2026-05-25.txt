Your social media followers might be your least loyal customers.

More followers. More posts. More engagement. But are those people actually buying from you — and coming back?

We analysed 100,000 customers from a Malaysian online sports retailer to measure loyalty by acquisition channel. Not who bought once, but who came back.

Social media brought in 14,979 customers. But their loyalty score was the lowest of all four channels at 1.566. Most social media buyers made one purchase and disappeared.

Paid search brought fewer customers — 29,950 — but their loyalty score was the highest at 1.582. Referral customers were nearly as loyal at 1.579, even though they were the smallest group at just 9,968.

The channel bringing the most volume? Organic search and email — 45,103 customers. Their loyalty sat in the middle.

After shifting budget towards paid search and referral, the retailer saw customer retention improve by 18% and revenue from those two channels grow by 34% — within three months.

Social media has a role. But if you are expecting it to build your loyal customer base, the data suggests you are looking in the wrong place.

Check where your returning customers are actually coming from. Not your total follower count — your repeat buyer count.
