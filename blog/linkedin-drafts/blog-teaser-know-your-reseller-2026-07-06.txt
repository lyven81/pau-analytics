Your highest-revenue reseller received $26,781 in discounts last year.
Their gross margin contribution: -$1,250.

Most manufacturers and distributors rank their reseller network by sales volume. The bigger the order, the more support, discounts, and attention they get. It feels logical. But revenue tells you how much a reseller is buying — not whether those transactions are actually making you money.

When a Malaysian biscuit and cookie manufacturer stopped ranking 635 resellers by revenue and started ranking by gross margin, the leaderboard looked nothing like before. The reseller receiving the most discount support was returning a loss on every single order. Meanwhile, 72% of the network — 460 partners — were contributing almost nothing to the company's profit.

The 65 Champions (just 10% of the network) were quietly carrying a disproportionate share of gross margin. Reseller 506 contributed $182,884 in gross margin at an 18% margin rate. One account the manufacturer had largely taken for granted.

If you manage a reseller or dealer network, the question worth asking this week: are your biggest discounts going to partners with the best margins — or just the biggest volumes?
