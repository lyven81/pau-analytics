We looked at 200 gym members to find who was most likely to quit.

Age was almost useless as a predictor.

Most gym owners assume demographics tell you something about retention — younger members are more committed, older ones more likely to fade out. The data said otherwise.

We analysed visit frequency, workout duration, calories burned, and age for every member. The numbers showed one thing clearly: how often someone shows up is what matters. The correlation between visit frequency and dropout was -0.85. That is a very strong signal.

Age? It barely moved the needle. Training habits turned out to be 30 times more predictive than how old a member was.

Two groups emerged from the data. Most members visited regularly — they were safe. A smaller group came in rarely and had inconsistent workouts. These members were already halfway out the door. The model predicted their departure with near-perfect accuracy because the pattern was so clear.

The middle group needed the most attention: members visiting 8 to 12 times a month. Losing just three visits in a week was a red flag.

If you run a membership business — gym, yoga studio, swim school — check your visit frequency data before doing anything else. You probably already have the signal. The question is whether you're reading it.
