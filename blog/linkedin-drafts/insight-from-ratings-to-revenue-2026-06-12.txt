Case Study: From Ratings to Revenue

---

Your Shopee product's future is decided at 30 reviews — not 1,000.

Most e-commerce sellers treat ratings as a vanity metric. Something to feel good about when it's high, but not something that directly explains what sells.

The data says otherwise.

When we analysed a Shopee product catalogue, we found an almost one-to-one match between the number of ratings a product received and its total units sold. But the most important finding was not about scale. It was about when the turning point happens.

Products with fewer than 30 ratings had unpredictable outcomes. Once a product crossed 30 to 50 ratings, the chance of it becoming a strong seller jumped to nearly 100 percent. The window between 20 and 30 reviews was where the odds began to shift sharply in the product's favour.

By the time a product reaches 1,000 ratings, it is already averaging around 2,400 units sold. At 10,000 ratings, that reaches roughly 23,000 units. But the critical inflection — the moment where buyer confidence and platform visibility align — happens far earlier than most sellers realise.

If you are launching products and spreading your promotion budget evenly across everything, you may be missing the window that actually matters.

Check which of your new products are sitting between 10 and 50 reviews right now. Those are the ones worth a focused push this week.
