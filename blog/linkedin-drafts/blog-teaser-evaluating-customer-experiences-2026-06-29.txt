Most retail managers think customer experience is a staffing problem.

The data says it is a management problem.

Specifically, a problem of knowing which stores actually need urgent attention — and which ones are fine.

A regional manager overseeing 43 stores across Malaysia had a sense that performance was uneven. But without data, it was impossible to know where to focus. When the numbers came back, the picture was stark: only 4 stores out of 43 were genuine statistical outliers. The worst location had a customer experience score of 32% — sitting nearly 38 points below the regional benchmark. It had been sitting there, quietly, while the manager was spreading attention across all 43 stores equally.

The analysis also showed that 75% of the worst underperformance came down to behaviour — how staff greeted customers, explained promotions, and handled consultations. Not broken systems. Behaviour. Which means it is fixable, within 6 to 8 weeks, with the right coaching.

But first you have to know which stores to focus on. And you have to know what kind of problem you are dealing with before you can solve it.

If you manage more than one location, or lead a team where performance varies between outlets, this breakdown is worth 5 minutes of your time.
