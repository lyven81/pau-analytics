The Four Leaks

Where the leak usually hides

Four places we find leaks most often:

1. Time-of-week labour mismatch

You're paying for staff hours that aren't matched to when customers actually come. Friday busy, Monday quiet — but payroll is flat.

2. Category margin drift

One category's margin has slipped 3–5% over 6 months. P&L doesn't catch it because total revenue is up. You're selling more of a lower-margin product.

3. Wholesale creep

Your supplier price went up 1–3% quarterly. Nobody re-negotiated. The cumulative effect over 12 months is meaningful.

4. Dead recurring spend

SaaS subscriptions, POS add-ons, software licences nobody's cancelling. Each RM50/mo feels nothing. Twenty of them = RM1,000/mo = RM12,000/year.

Proof

Friday Through Sunday

F&B CLIENT, MALAYSIA

"Friday is our best day. But somehow it's also the most expensive. Why?"

We analyzed 12 months of POS data + payroll + inventory. Friday revenue was 3.2× Monday — but Friday cost was 4.1× Monday. Labour scheduling was flat across the week. Friday inventory spoilage was 2× the weekly average because of over-prep.

Result: Shifted labour to match revenue shape (40% Friday weight, 10% Monday weight). Prep quantity keyed to 3-week rolling average, not flat daily. Net savings: 11% of weekly operating cost.
