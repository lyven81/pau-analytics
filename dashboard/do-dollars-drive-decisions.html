Baseline at a Glance
Typical approved loan sits in the $10K–$20K range at an 8–14% rate. Grades B and C dominate the book, and borrowers earning $40K–$100K are the largest cohort. Use this as the baseline when evaluating a new application.

Officer Takeaway

  • Median approved loan: ~$15K; median rate: ~12%.
  • Top 4 states (CA, NY, TX, FL) account for over one-third of the book.
  • If an application falls outside these baselines, flag it for second review.

Risk Takeaways

  • About 4% of the book sits in high-risk grades E/F/G — watch these closely.
  • Credit utilization jumps from 31% (Grade A) to 49%+ (Grades D–F) — a strong warning signal.
  • High DTI and high loan-to-income combinations cluster in grades D–F — flag both dimensions at intake.

Fairness Takeaways

  • Rates within the same grade are nearly identical across income groups (within 0.3 percentage points) — pricing looks consistent.
  • However, loan amounts differ meaningfully: high-income borrowers receive ~$8K more at the same grade. Confirm this is driven by credit capacity, not income alone.
  • Grade mix skews: 59.6% of high-income borrowers land in A/B vs 51.1% of low-income. Review the grading model for bias.
